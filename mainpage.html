Steven's Story

Steven Barrier was born and raised in Stamford, Connecticut. He was a great member of our community - he grew, laughed, and loved here. On October 23rd, 2019, on his 23rd birthday, Steven was apprehended by the police and denied immediate medical attention, resulting in his death. That night Stamford PD failed the community they are sworn to protect. Justice for Steven Barrier was formed as a community collective seeking to advocate for change in how Stamford treats its residents. Our number one demand from our group is firing and arrest of the police officers showing no regard for Stevenâ€™s humanity, which lead to his death. However, we understand these atrocities do not happen in a vacuum. Steven was diagnosed with schizophrenia in 2015. We demand full funding of the state mental health facilities so no one is denied proper care or space when necessary. Our second demand, for the city of Stamford, is to establish a mental health response team (more words/description).Funding and staffing for Stamford public services have been declining for several decades, and the past ten years have seen a ruthless acceleration in this trend.This violence from austerity displays itself in our government in numerous forms. Some examples include the delayed maintenance of public schools which led to a widespread mold crisis starting in 2018 and forced students into new learning environments, allowing multibillion dollar corporate landlords and developers access to land to use how they please - including building luxury apartments that directly displace lower income locals, and over-staffing and over-supplying the Stamford police department with new gear, more hours, more control, and higher pay. This is why we say Defund Police! They do not contribute to the well-being of our community, and any civic functions they serve, could easily be transferred to other existing, or new, well-payed folks who do not march into work with bullet proof.
